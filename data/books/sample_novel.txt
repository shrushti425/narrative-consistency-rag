Arjun grew up in the small coastal town of Devgarh, where life moved slowly and traditions were rarely questioned. His father, a fisherman, believed deeply in self-reliance and taught Arjun from a young age that asking for help was a sign of weakness. When Arjun was twelve, his father was injured in a storm and could no longer go to sea. From that moment on, Arjun became unusually cautious, avoiding risks and placing safety above ambition.

During his teenage years, Arjun excelled academically but refused opportunities that required him to leave Devgarh. He turned down a scholarship to a distant university, choosing instead to attend a local college so he could support his family. Friends often described him as dependable but fearful of change. He distrusted authority figures, especially government officials, believing they made promises they never intended to keep.

In his early twenties, a major industrial project was announced near Devgarh. The project promised jobs but also threatened the fishing economy. Arjun initially opposed it, organizing peaceful protests and writing letters to local representatives. However, when his mother fell ill and medical expenses mounted, Arjun reluctantly accepted a job offered by the same corporation he once protested against.

Working within the company changed him in subtle ways. He became more pragmatic, less idealistic, and increasingly willing to compromise his beliefs for stability. By the time Devgarh transformed into an industrial hub, Arjun was no longer the risk-averse boy he once was. Yet, traces of his upbringing remained—he still avoided public leadership roles and never fully trusted those in power, even when he became one of them.
